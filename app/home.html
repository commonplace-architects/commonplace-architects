Common Place Make a change in your community

Who are we?

We are a Manchester based collaborative practice who puts community interest at the forefront of all we do. We act as a mediator between those who own land and groups who are looking to realise a community focused project. Positioning ourselves at the convergence of architecture and real-estate, we are a platform which allows groups within communities to match their design ideas to specific sites identified by us, supporting the project through construction to post-occupancy. We identify the potential of brownfield sites, such land which is often overlooked or dismissed, to realise these community focused projects.

What is the issue?

In the past years, land value has risen due to public investment, however, this is only economically benefiting land owners and developers. Governments are finding it difficult to identify the needs of individual places and communities. Consequently, many buildings are left unused during a large proportion of the day.

How we solve this?

At Common Place, we strongly believe in a democratic land ownership system. We are revolutionising how the economy behind land ownership works. We want to provide opportunities to communities which would otherwise be outbid, or who would not otherwise know how to purchase and develop the project themselves. With community interest at the very centre of all we do as a practice, we believe an understanding of the areas in which we work and the communities which we work with is key to realising successful projects. We believe in having full support in the principles to which the community groups we work with. Allowing us, as a practice, to be fully invested in the projects. we therefore are pushing for an increase in the usage of buildings by community groups. For example, to use buildings in the evening for funding events. Social inclusion of all groups and minorities within the community. Giving communities the opportunity to decide what happens to the area that surrounds them. As a practice, we look towards a more sustainable future, where a circular economy can be achieved. Though seeing the potential in derelict brownfield sites, we believe the growth of cities can happen without using greenfield land as the urban population grows and urban sprawl occurs at an increasing rate.
